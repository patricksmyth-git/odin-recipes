Beef Wellington

Description

Named after the Duke of Wellington, the Englishman who defeated Napolean, Beef Wellington is a famous English dish.

Similar dishes most likely predate Beef Wellington by centuries.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef tenderloin filet
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons eltra virgin olive oil
  • 1 pound mushrooms (cremini & shiitake mix)
  • 4 thin slices ham (Parma ham if you can get it) or prosciutto
  • 2 tablespoons yellow mustard (we used Coleman's Original English Mustard)
  • 1 (7 to 8 1/2-ounce sheet) puff pastry, thawed (see recipe note)
  • 2 large egg yolks, beaten

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F as you assemble the Wellington.
  2. Season the fillet generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat a tablespoon or two of oil in a large pan on high heat.
  4. Sear the fillet in the pan on all sides until well browned. (Do not move the fillet until it is browned.)
  5. Remove the fillet from the pan & let it cool.
  6. Brush the fillet on all sides with mustard.
  7. Chop the mushrooms. Purée them in a food processor.
  8. Heat the saute pan on medium-high heat.
  9. Add the mushroom purée to the pan and cook, allowing the mushrooms to release their moisture.
  10. Once the moisture has been boiled away, remove the mushrooms from the pan and set them aside to cool.
  11. Roll out a large piece of plastic wrap.
  12. Lay out slices of ham on the plastic wrap so that they overlap.
  13. Spread the mushroom mixture over the ham.
  14. Place the beef fillet in the middle of the ham & mushroom base.
  15. Roll the mushroom and ham base over the fillet, using the plastic wrap to do this tightly.
  16. Wrap the beef fillet into a tight barrel shape, twisting the ends of the plastic wrap to secure. Refrigerate this for 20 minutes.
  17. On a lightly-floured surface, roll out the puff pastry to a size that will wrap around the beef fillet.
  18. Unwrap the fillet from the plastic wrap and place it in the middle of the pastry dough.
  19. Brush the edges of the pastry with the beaten egg yolks.
  20. Fold the pastry around the fillet. Cut off any excess pastry. Try not to overlap the pastry much.
  21. Place the fillet on a small plate, seam-side down, and brush with beaten egg yolks all over the top.
  22. Chill the fillet for 5-10 minutes.
  23. Put the fillet on a baking pan. Brush the exposed surface again with beaten eggs.
  24. Score the top of the pastry with a sharp knife (DO NOT GO ALL THE WAY THROUGH THE PASTRY.)
  25. Sprinkle the top with coarse salt.
  26. Bake in the 400 degree oven for 25-35 minutes. The pastry will be golden when finished.
  27. Test the meat with an instant-read thermometer. Take the Wellington out of the oven at 125-130 degrees for medium-rare.
  28. Let rest for 10 minutes before slicing.
  29. Slice in 1-inch thick slices and serve.
